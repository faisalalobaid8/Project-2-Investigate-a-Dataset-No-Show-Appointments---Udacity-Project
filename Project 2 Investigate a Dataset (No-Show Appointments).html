Introduction¶

The No-Show Appointments dataset includes more than 110 thousands recoreds of medical appointments in Brazil. These medical appointments recoreds are collected form 62 thousands patients, which means that the patient could have more than one medical appointment recored. This dataset is focused on the question of whether the patients are going to show up for their appointment or not. Since this is the focused of the dataset, the dependent variable will be the column which represents whether the patients showed up or not

Conclusions¶

In conclusion, the No-Show Appointments dataset was almost cleaned. I only have found few inaccurate data, such as inaccurate type of data, negative value in age column, and some recored have the Appointment Day came before the Scheduled Day, which seems to be wrong data. All of these inaccurate data were fixed before analyzing the data.

After analyzing the dataset, we are able to identity the factors, which will help us to know in order to predict whether a patient will show up for their scheduled appointment or not. The factors are the the age of the patient and whether the paitent suffer from diseases, such as diabetes and hipertension or not. We can concluded from the analysis that the older patient will most likely to show up for their appointments. Also, patients who suffer from diabetes or hypertension will less likely to skip the medical appointment.

Limitation¶

As it is mentioned in the conclusion section, the No-Show Appointments dataset was almost cleaned. The dataset does not have any missing value and does not have any duplicate recoreds. Also, the data only have few outliers, which does not affect the final predictions at all. Therefore, while i was analyzing the dataset, I did not face any limitation.
